❓ Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is a menstrual cycle?
    It's a monthly hormonal cycle that prepares your body for pregnancy. If no pregnancy occurs, you get your period.
  2. How long does a period usually last?
    Most periods last 3–7 days.
  3. What is a normal cycle length?
    Typically 21 to 35 days, but it varies from person to person.
  4. Is it normal to have cramps?
    Yes! Mild to moderate cramps are common and usually caused by the uterus contracting.
  5. Can stress affect my period?
    Definitely. High stress can delay or even skip your period.
  6. What are PMS symptoms?
    Mood swings, bloating, cravings, and fatigue before your period are common PMS symptoms.
  7. Is it okay to exercise during my period?
    Yes, light exercise like walking or yoga can actually help reduce cramps and boost your mood!
  8. Can I swim while on my period?
    Totally! Just use a tampon or menstrual cup.
  9. When should I see a doctor?
    If your period is extremely painful, irregular, or you miss multiple cycles, talk to a doctor.
  10. Can I get pregnant during my period?
    It's rare, but still possible if you have a short cycle. Always use protection if you're not trying to conceive.

📚 Menstrual Health Facts & Myths

🩸 Ancient cultures considered menstruating women sacred In some cultures, menstruating women were seen as spiritually powerful and given special status.
📅 You can still get pregnant on your period Sperm can live up to 5 days, so pregnancy is still possible if ovulation overlaps.
😌 Periods aren’t supposed to be super painful Mild discomfort is normal, but severe pain may be a sign of endometriosis or other issues.
🍫 PMS cravings are real (and chocolate helps!) Hormonal shifts trigger cravings — magnesium in chocolate may help with cramps too!
🚫 You don’t “lose” a lot of blood On average, you lose just 2–3 tablespoons of blood during your period.
🌕 Some believe periods sync with the moon While not proven, some people report cycle alignment with lunar phases!
🛁 Baths are totally safe during periods There's no reason to avoid baths — they can actually help soothe cramps.
🤯 “Period brain” is backed by science Hormonal changes *can* affect memory and focus — it’s not just in your head!
🩱 Menstrual cups are safe & eco-friendly They’re reusable, save money, and reduce waste compared to pads/tampons.
💬 Talking about periods is important Breaking the stigma helps everyone feel more informed and supported.
